# Rewrite Checklist

Use this checklist for every polishing task.

## Meaning Preservation

- Preserve all numeric values, units, sample sizes, time points, doses, percentages, and statistical qualifiers.
- Preserve all citations exactly unless the user asks for citation formatting.
- Preserve names of genes, proteins, cell types, tissues, organs, diseases, models, and assays.
- Preserve whether the evidence is in vitro, ex vivo, in vivo, preclinical, clinical, computational, or review-based.
- Preserve the original strength of claims. Do not convert `may improve` into `improves`, or `suggests` into `demonstrates`.

## Anti-AI Polish

- Remove generic throat-clearing and overbroad setup.
- Replace vague praise with specific biological or formulation-level meaning.
- Shorten stacked adjectives unless each adjective adds scientific information.
- Prefer active, experiment-linked verbs: `enabled`, `reduced`, `increased`, `redirected`, `enhanced`, `attenuated`, `supported`, `revealed`.
- Keep sentence rhythm varied: one compact topic sentence, one mechanistic sentence, then one evidence or implication sentence often reads better than a uniformly long paragraph.

## Cheng-Wei English Prose Register

- Prefer concrete sentence subjects: `The results`, `This strategy`, `These findings`, `We`, or the specific object being studied.
- Prefer exact verbs: `developed`, `designed`, `engineered`, `evaluated`, `enabled`, `enhanced`, `revealed`, `confirmed`, `suggested`.
- Keep most sentences near 18-30 words; split sentences that carry several claims at once.
- Balance active and passive voice. Use active voice for author actions and passive voice for methods or measurements.
- Use transitions sparingly: `However` for gaps, `Here/Herein` for contribution, `Notably` for key observations, and `Together/Overall` for bounded conclusions.
- End paragraphs with a restrained inference rather than a broad promotional statement.

## Final Self-Check

Before returning a rewritten paragraph, ask:

- Did I add a claim that was not in the input?
- Did I strengthen or weaken uncertainty?
- Did I change the model system, endpoint, payload, or target cell?
- Did I keep citations attached to the same claims?
- Does the revised text sound like a precise scientific paragraph rather than a promotional summary?
